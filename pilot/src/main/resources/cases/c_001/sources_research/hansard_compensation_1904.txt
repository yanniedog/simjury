Wrongful Conviction—Compensation for Adolf Beck.
HC Deb 03 August 1904 vol 139 cc719-20

(Donegal, S.) asked whether compensation would be made to Adolf Beck,
who in 1895 was tried, convicted, and sentenced to seven years penal
servitude at the Old Bailey for a crime of which his innocence has now
been conclusively established, and who recently has been tried and
convicted for a crime of which his innocence has been established in the
interval between conviction and sentence, both convictions having been
obtained on evidence of Beck's identity, which was mistaken.

(Akers-Douglas, Kent, St. Augustine's) replied that he was in
communication with the Treasury regarding the grant of a sum of money to
Mr. Adolf Beck as compensation for his two mistaken convictions. Beck
had already received from the King His Majesty's free pardon, the effect
of which entirely wipes out the conviction and "obliterates every stain"
which the law attaches to the alleged offence.
